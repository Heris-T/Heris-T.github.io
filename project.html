Business Disaster Recovery Plan
1. Identify potential threats: Determine the types of disasters or interruptions that could occur, such as natural disasters, power outages, or cyber attacks.
2. Assess the impact: Consider the potential impact of each identified threat on your business operations, including financial loss, loss of customers, and damage to reputation.
3. Develop a plan: Create a detailed plan that outlines the steps your business will take to respond to and recover from each identified threat. This should include procedures for emergency response, data backup and recovery, and communication with employees and customers.
4. Test and update the plan: Regularly test and update the plan to ensure it remains effective and relevant.
5. Document and Communicate: Document the plan and communicate it to all relevant personnel so that everyone knows their roles and responsibilities in the event of a disaster.
6. Review and Update: Regularly review and update the plan to ensure it remains effective and relevant.
ISO standard for information security
ISO 27001 is the international standard for establishing, implementing, maintaining and improving an information security management system (ISMS). It provides the requirements for organizations to protect their digital assets from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, destruction or modification.
ISO 27002 is a supplementary guide to ISO 27001 that provides guidance on the implementation of the security controls listed in Annex A of ISO 27001. It outlines the requirements for selecting and implementing security controls that help organizations ensure the confidentiality, integrity and availability of their digital assets.
ISO 22301 is an international standard that provides organizations with a framework for establishing and maintaining a business continuity management system (BCMS). It outlines the requirements for an effective BCMS and helps organizations plan, implement and maintain their BCMS.
ISO 27005 is an international standard that outlines the procedures for conducting an information security risk assessment in compliance with ISO 27001. It provides guidance to organizations on how to properly identify, assess and respond to risks.
